<p><p>The EDLC supercapacitor market primarily comprises two types: double-layer capacitors and pseudocapacitors. Double-layer capacitors store energy via electrostatic charge separation at the interface between an electrode and electrolyte, allowing for rapid charge and discharge cycles. Pseudocapacitors, on the other hand, utilize electrochemical processes along with charge separation to achieve higher capacitance and energy density. Both types serve diverse applications, including energy storage in renewable systems, automotive technologies, and consumer electronics, driven by the need for efficient energy management solutions.</p></p>
